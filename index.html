Step 1 of 6

Catching the Horse

The first step in succesfully saddling your horse is to catch him first!

Walk out into the field with a treat, halter and lead rope. Catch your horse feed them the treat and walk them back to the barn.

Part 2 of 6

Brushing the Horse

Now that you have caught your horse its time to clean them up!

Grab a brush and start brushing! Dont forget to pick their feet and brush their main and tail!

  • Previous
  • Next

Part 3 of 6

Putting on the Saddle Pad

Now that your horse is shiny it time to start the big steps! Go and grab your saddle Pad

Put the saddle pad on the horse so that the velcro keepers are closest to the horses head! Place the pad so it sits evenly on both sides and is sitting about 2 inches from where their mane ends in the middle of their withers!

  • Previous
  • Next

Part 4 of 6

Putting on the Saddle

Now that you have on the saddle pad you are almost their!

Grab the saddle and have it so that the wider part is near the back of the horse and the narrow part is at the front of the horse, place the saddle on top of the saddle pad and reposition it to be about 2 inches back from the front of the saddle pad.

  • Next

Part 5 of 6

Attatching the keepers

Now to attach the Velcro Keepers onto the saddle!

Open up the Velcro Keepers from one another and attach it to the first billet on the saddle and secure veclro around the billet.

  • Previous
  • Next

Part 6 of 6

Doing up the Girth

Congradulations your on the final step!

Now you need go get your girth and do it up! Start doing up the girth always on the right side of the horse! Start with the non elastic side of the girth.

Place the girth on the first hole, then go to the other side do it up and adjust accordinglee.

NOTE: Always check the girth before mounting as horses bloat and the girth may need tightening after walking to the ring!

Enjoy your Ride!
